Model Overview & Intended Use

DeepSeek-R1 represents a landmark transition in open-weights artificial intelligence. Released in January 2025, it competes directly with frontier closed-source reasoning systems (such as OpenAI's o1) in complex logic, math, and code generation.

By leveraging reinforcement learning (RL) to enforce thinking paths, the model learns to self-correct, cross-examine hypotheses, and output a highly detailed thinking process before presenting final answers.

Training Datasets & Size

DeepSeek-R1's training corpus is built upon a multi-stage pipeline designed to ensure logical soundness while maintaining general helpfulness and safe formatting.

  • Cold-Start Dataset (SFT Stage 1): Several thousand highly curated, step-by-step reasoning prompts annotated with structural thinking delimiters (<think>...</think>) to prime the base model.
  • Reasoning Rejection Sampling (SFT Stage 2): 800,000 reasoning sequences generated across sciences, mathematics, and software engineering. These were automatically checked for correctness by compilers and compilers filters.
  • Non-Reasoning SFT Dataset: 200,000 general instruction samples covering creative writing, translation, simple chat dialogues, and safety alignments.
  • Distillation Corpus: DeepSeek used the filtered 800k high-quality reasoning files to train smaller networks directly (such as Qwen and Llama), avoiding the massive compute costs of raw RL training on lower parameters.

Training Parameters & Methodology

Rather than using standard PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization) which requires storing a separate, memory-intensive critic model, DeepSeek developed GRPO (Group Relative Policy Optimization).

For a given prompt, the model generates a group of outputs. The relative advantages of each output are calculated within the group to optimize weights, resulting in a 90%+ compute savings over traditional actor-critic setups.

Group Size (G)

Typically 8-16 generated candidate answers per prompt.

Reward Models

Compiler test cases, rule checkers for math correctness, and format reward markers.

Hardware Config

Trained across a massive cluster of H800 GPUs, achieving optimal communication speed.

Architecture: MLA & DeepSeekMoE

DeepSeek-R1 employs two primary architectural breakthroughs that reduce memory utilization and inference latency:

  • Multi-head Latent Attention (MLA): Projects Key and Value matrices down into a 512-dimension latent vector, decreasing KV cache size by up to 93% compared to traditional MHA.
  • DeepSeekMoE (Mixture-of-Experts): Divides layers into shared experts and routed experts. Totaling 671 billion parameters, it dynamically routes tokens so only 37 billion active parameters are processed per token, matching the inference cost of a mid-sized dense model.

Limitations & Boundary Conditions

While incredibly capable, researchers and developers should note the following boundary conditions:

  • Language Mixing: R1 can occasionally experience slight mixing (e.g. outputting Chinese characters within English technical paths) in highly specific multi-turn contexts.
  • Conversational Delay: For simple conversational greetings (e.g., "Hello"), the model can trigger unnecessary long-thinking sequences unless specifically throttled.
  • Verbosity: The `` outputs can be extremely verbose, requiring custom parsers or streaming implementations on front-ends to hide them.
